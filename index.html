Explore Family Tree Our Temple
Our Origins

A Journey of 250 Years

From Kancheepuram to Coimbatore

Our forebears were among the devotees of the great temple city of Kancheepuram — one of the seven sacred cities of India — where the tradition of faith, learning, and community was deeply woven into daily life.

Around 1775, the Nallaveerappa-Mudaliyar family made the momentous migration southward to the fertile Coimbatore district, drawn by opportunity and guided by destiny. They established their home base in Coimbatore and gradually settled in Chittur, where they would leave their greatest mark.

True to their heritage, the family's first act of permanence in their new land was the construction of a house of worship — the Sree Meenakshi Sudareswara Temple — a gift to the community that endures to this day.

Sree Meenakshi Sudareswara Temple

🛕

SREE MEENAKSHI
SUDARESWARA TEMPLE

Chittur, Coimbatore District

A Temple of Exceptional Power

🧭

South-Facing Goddess

Uniquely, Goddess Meenakshi faces South — the direction of Yama, the lord of death — indicating immense divine power. South-facing deities are considered exceptionally potent blessings of protection.

🪷

Divine Consorts

Sree Meenakshi — the fish-eyed goddess of beauty and grace — is enshrined alongside Lord Sundareswara (Shiva), making this a powerful Shakti-Shaiva temple of dual blessings.

🏛️

Family Heritage Site

Built by the Nallaveerappa-Mudaliyar family as an act of devotion upon settling in Chittur. The temple stands as the spiritual cornerstone of the family's 250-year legacy in Coimbatore.
